DALMATION

WHAT IS IT?

The dignified Dalmatian, dogdom's citizen of the world, is famed
for his spotted coat and unique job description.The Dalmatian's
delightful, eye-catching spots of black or liver adorn one of the
most distinctive coats in the animal kingdom.

ORIGIN

The Dalmatian's true origins are up for debate, but evidence of similar types of dogs goes
back to ancient times. It is likely that the breed did not actually originate in Dalmatia
(present-day Croatia), but it is certain that Dalmatians were once used as sentinels in
the area. The Dalmatian is known for its affinity with horses, which made it the original
and prized carriage dog or coach dog in England.

TEMPERAMENT

The temperament of a dalmatian tends to be loving, energetic, and athletic. Because of their
high energy levels, they need an owner that is able to take them out on a regular basis and
that can give them an active lifestyle. Another important thing to note is that Dalmatians
love to be around their owners. Whether you’re cooking in the kitchen or lounging on the
couch, you can expect your Dalmatian to be right by your side wherever you go.

APPEARANCE

The Dalmatian is a medium-to-large sized dog with a lean, muscular, proportionate build that is as long
as it is high.Dalmatians weigh approximately 23-25 cm; the male dog tends to be slightly taller (58-61 cm)
than the female dog (56-58 cm).The body is as long from fore-chest to buttocks as it is tall at the
withers, and the shoulders are well-laid back, the stifle is well-bent, and the hocks are well-let-down.

STEPS TO FOLLOW

  • Good preparation creates a smoother transition into dog ownership. Make sure you have a plan in mind for who is going to care for your pup during the day if you work full time.

LIFESPAN

The average lifespan for a Dalmation is about 10 to 15 years.

DISEASES THAT DALMATION GETS AFFECTED

  • Deafness: About 10 to 12 percent of Dalmatians are born deaf and 22 to 24 percent have hearing only in one ear.
  • Hip Dysplasia: This is an inherited condition that can produce lameness and arthritis.
  • Urinary Issues (infections, stones):They need plenty of water and food that is not high in purines.
  • Skin Allergies: Dalmatians may have food-based allergies, contact allergies, or inhalant allergies.

HEALTH CARE

This breed requires little more than basic grooming.Be sure to clean the Dalmatian's ears regularly to prevent ear infections. In addition, keep the nails neatly trimmed so your Dalmatian can walk around comfortably and safely.Dalmatians need tons of exercise and plenty of activities. Otherwise, they are prone to weight gain, anxiety, and even behavior problems.

FOOD NUTRITION

Feed your Dalmatian a low-purine diet: Purines are a substance that, when not metabolized properly, contribute to the formation of urate crystals in your Dalmatian’s urinary tract.

Establish a feeding schedule for your Dalmatian. If you have a Dalmatian puppy, feed him 3 times a day.

Give your Dalmatian unlimited access to fresh water.Since Dalmatians are prone to developing urinary stones, they need to drink water constantly to flush out their urinary system.
